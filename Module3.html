MODULE 3

SECURITY & RISK MANAGEMENT

Group-Contract

  • Group-Contract

    • Unit-1: Introduction to Security and Risk Management

    Knowledge:
    1. Review a number of common definitions of Security and Risk Management
    2. Review some common Security and Risk Management Concepts.
    3. Discuss the Risk Management Process.

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Discuss the various definitions of risk.
    2. Explain how to assess, qualify and mitigate risks.
    3. Describe various approaches to quantify and qualify risks.
    4. List common security and risk standards and select the appropriate one(s) for a given situation.

    Unit-2: Risk Management Process - (Qualitative vs. Quantitative)

    Knowledge:
    1. Review the Risk Management Process (RMP)
    2. Review the effects of different assessment types (Qualitative vs. Quantitative)
    3. Discuss potential changes to the RMP based on changes in user participation

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Discuss the differences between assessment approaches (Qualitative vs. Quantitative)
    2. Explain the importance of user participation in the risk management process
    3. Discuss the implications of any recommended mitigations

    Unit-3: Intro to Threat Modelling and Management

    Knowledge:
    1. Discuss the difference between threats and vulnerabilities
    2. Review a number of threat modelling techniques
    3. Discuss a number of guides and cookbooks to help with threat modelling

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Describe a number of threat modelling techniques
    2. Advise which technique should be used in specific situations
    3. Discuss when techniques should be combined in a hybrid model

    • Unit-4: Application of Threat Modelling and Management Techniques

    Knowledge:
    1. Discuss various tools and resources that can be used in threat modelling and management
    2. Evaluate the use of publicly available tools to assist with Threat Modelling
    3. Create threat models based on suggested scenarios

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Describe where to find the key tools and resources used for threat modelling
    2. Explain how to select the most appropriate tool for various situations
    3. Critically evaluate the use of such tools

    • Unit-5: Introduction to Security and Risk Standards

    Knowledge:
    1. Explore industry specific security standards and directives
    2. Discuss how standards affect security and risk assessments
    3. Review a number of common industry and enterprise standards

    Learning Outcome:
     1. List common security standards and select the appropriate one(s) for a given situation
    2. Describe how to allow for standards in typical threat models

    • Unit-6: Practical Implications of Security and Risk Standards

    Knowledge:
    1. Review and discuss a number of GDPR related case studies
    2. Review standards web sites
    3. Discuss a number of case studies and standards

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Explain which GDPR regulations are applicable in specific situations
    2. Describe which other standards might apply in selected situations
    3. Advise on mitigations to help a business meet any of the standards applicable to its specific industry (such as data and privacy (GDPR) or financial (PCI-DSS))

    DEVELOPMENT TEAM PROJECT-1 (Unit-6)

  • Risk Identification Report

    • Unit-7: Introduction to the Concepts of Quantitative Risk Modelling

    Knowledge:
    1. Discuss what is meant by quantitative risk modelling (QRM)
    2. Review different approaches to QRM including Monte Carlo Simulations and Bayes theoretical models
    3. Discuss the principles and antipatterns for each approach

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Utilise quantitative risk modelling techniques to produce risk models
    2. Select the most appropriate technique based on the real-world problem
    3. Critically evaluate the pros and cons of the approach selected

    Unit-8: Implementing Quantitative Risk Models

    Knowledge:
    1. Discuss how QRM can be applied to real world problems
    2. Review case studies of how QRM techniques are used
    3. Provide a critical evaluation of some of the techniques available

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Select the most appropriate modelling technique to use in a given situation
    2. Create a QR model of a real-world situation
    3. Provide a critical appraisal of the use of QR techniques

    Unit-9: Risk, Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

    Knowledge:
    1. Review how and why to create business continuity (BC) and disaster recovery (DR) plans
    2. Discuss the main determining factors in BC/DR plans – including Business Impact Assessments (BIA), Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs)
    3. Discuss emerging trends in Information Risk Management

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Explain what is meant by BC, DR, RTO and RPO
    2. Suggest the most suitable solutions to meet specific recovery objectives
    3. Discuss some of the factors driving the future of the Information Risk Management field

    Unit-10: Practical Applications and Issues in DR Implementations

    Knowledge:
    1. Discuss the impact of RPO and RTO values on DR solutions
    2. Examine some typical system solutions to meet the various standby requirements
    3. Describe the limitations of the proposed solutions

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Design a solution that will meet a set of RPO and RTO requirements
    2. Discuss the challenges with vendor lock-in, resilience and (network) security
    3. Describe the advantages and disadvantages of DRaaS

    Unit-11 & 12: Future Trends in Security and Risk Management

    Knowledge:
    1. Review a number of current and emerging trends in SRM
    2. Discuss the pros and cons of each trend
    3. Evaluate each trend in respect of its influence on future directions
    4. The Great Debate: What will be the most influential trend in SRM in the next 5 years?

    Learning Outcome:
     1. Describe the main emerging trends in the field
    2. Explain their potential impact on research
    3. Recommend which you think will be most influential
